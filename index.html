I am a Ph.D. candidate in the ECE department at at University of Southern California. I am co-advised by Prof. Ramesh Govindan and Prof. Barath Raghavan at Networked System Lab (NSL). Prior to joining USC, I received my B.S. degree in EECS and B.A. degree in Economics from Peking University.

I am interested in Computer Networking and System research including software-defined networking (SDN), network function virtualization (NFV), and high-performance cloud/edge computing. I enjoy building large-scale, high-throughput and low-latency computing systems, especially for packet-processing systems.

I've built Lemur, a high-throuhgput NFV cross-platform compiler; Quadrant, a cloud-deployable NFV platform with an efficient isolation mechanism; Ironside, a rack-scale scheduler that provides latency guarantees for NFV; PGPP, a privacy-preserving proxy (one example of NFV app) for Android (free download @ Google Play Store). PGPP can hide your IP address when you use web services (see this Wired report for more info).
